Week 5: Publish Data

Today the scientific community is as global and open as ever. This positive development is not last owing to research ethics. In particular, the principle of transparency in sources and methods, and the principlle of data reproducibility.

It is also worthy of noting that, as researchers are often employed by companies, they are often pressured to publish their research in a way that benefits the company, which is not always ethical or scientific. For example, the case of Jose Baselga, a doctor who didn't disclose his conflict of interest with many pharmaceutical companies.

And, this may seem obvious, but publishing AI generated papers without disclosing the use of AI is unethical, because it is a form of plagiarism and potential misinformation. In my opinion, the world needs more original research. Especially when AI content is everpresent.

Although, eventually, as time passes, we reach a certain stage when published data has two ways to go: down into the garbage or up in the archives.
