Week 6: Preservation and Destruction of Data

Why would we want to destroy data?

As the lifecycle of a data is nearing its end (data becomes outdated, not queried enough, or it contains sensitive personal data), the people or organizations responsible for managing the data may choose to destroy it. This can be done for various reasons, such as to protect privacy, comply with legal requirements, or simply to free up storage space. Of course, the methods depend on the medium used and, even when data is buried, it is mandatory to leave the metadata intact to ensure the data can be properly cited and attributed in the future, even if the data itself is no longer accessible.

On the other hand, there is a novel concept of the right to be forgotten. Increasingly more service providers include an option that allows users to choose what happens with their data upon their death. This can be seen as a progressive development in data management, as it means more autonomy and control for users over their data.
