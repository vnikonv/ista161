Week 1: Introduction to Ethics and Digitalization

What have we been dealing with in the first week?

An important concept I've learned over the first week of this course is the distinction between digitization and digitalization. Digitization is the process of converting analog information into digital format, while digitalization refers to the broader societal and organizational changes that occur as a result of adopting digital technologies. In my first post, I have demonstrated a VHS tape as an example of an analog carrier as opposed to a digital one, like an HDD. This is digitization.

As for digitalization, I have discussed the example of the e-government portal of Kazakhstan, which has transformed how citizens interact with government services. And the comic with the caption "help! im being held accountable" is a humorous take on how data hoarding entities complain about accountability and actively resist regulations that would hold them accountable for their data practices. This is a common issue in the digital age, where companies often prioritize profit over ethical considerations, urging us to meditate on the importance of certain guidelines in managing data ethically.

However, before anything can be done with data, it must be acquired first.
